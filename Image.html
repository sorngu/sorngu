This residential area was previously a housing complex for the families of employees of the petroleum system. It was established to address the housing needs of the staff of Bohai Petroleum Company and related construction and engineering systems. Therefore, the early residents were mostly petroleum workers, construction engineers and their families.



China National Offshore Oil Corporation - Bohai Branch



Internal roads of Jian Gong New Village



The community buildings feature distinct Soviet aesthetic elements and the industrial style of the 1970s and 1980s. Most of the buildings are multi-story brick-concrete structures. The exterior often features gray or beige prefabricated panels, and the balconies are equipped with blue plastic steel windows or iron railings.

Binhai New Area District Fishing Village Port

The port was established in 1988.

Port of Entry , 2026

4032 x 3024 mm



The 1988 concrete shoreline, gnawed by salt particles into a matte white. The blue hull's paint peels to reveal cobalt blue amid the gray tones; the bright red of the red flag is the only burst of color--pinned against the cloudless sky, folding the shape of the wind into wrinkles. A plastic basin on the empty deck grows grass, a sliver of green borrowed by humans from the tidal flat; the anchor chain coils into a metallic rest, twisting time into an unbreakable knot together with the foxtail grass sprouting from the cracks in the dispatch building's walls. No fishing songs, only the salty sea breeze lingering over the entire port.

Fisherman's Port , 2026

6613 x 4225 mm



First of all, let's talk about the flower cloth on the iron fence - the red background was pulled out of soft folds by the wind, like the wedding quilt that had not been collected on the fishing boat was lent to the beach for decoration. The orange petals in the flower print were stained with grass shavings, and the broken green of the wall was entangled into an irregular warmth. Looking at the wooden boat moored in the gray water, the wood grain of the hull was immersed, as if it had absorbed the sound of the tide from 1988 to the present into the texture. The red flag in the distance floated very lightly in the gray tone, separated from the red of the flower cloth, becoming the two points echoing in time and space. Finally, it fell back to the empty railing on the shore, and the rusty gnawed the texture of the wood, like the words that were not finished stuck in the salt wind, waiting for the next wave to continue.
